TASK: Source Vendors for Government Solicitation

Objective
Identify and compile a list of vendors capable of fulfilling the scope of a government solicitation. Before sourcing, first classify the scope of work to determine whether geographic proximity is required or irrelevant — then apply the appropriate search strategy.

Step 1: Geographic Classification (Do This First)
Before searching for any vendors, read the scope of work and classify it into one of two categories:
🔴 Location-Dependent Scope (Local Vendors Only)
Apply the 200-mile radius filter when the work requires physical presence at the place of performance. This includes:
Installation, construction, or demolition at a site
Roofing, flooring, painting, or facility repairs
Rigging, equipment moving, or on-site assembly
Hazmat disposal or environmental remediation at a location
HVAC, plumbing, electrical, or mechanical services
Any scope where a technician, crew, or equipment must travel to and remain at the job site
Rule: If the vendor must show up in person to do the work, restrict your search to within 200 miles of the place of performance.

🟢 Location-Agnostic Scope (National Vendors Eligible)
Do not apply a geographic filter when the scope can be fulfilled remotely or via shipping. This includes:
Products that can be shipped (e.g., canopy tents, electronics, furniture, supplies, equipment)
Software, digital services, or remote consulting
Manufacturing or fabrication of goods that are then delivered
Staffing or professional services performed remotely
Any scope where the vendor never needs to visit the place of performance
Rule: If the vendor can ship a product or perform the work without being on-site, search nationally and do not restrict by radius. This maximizes competition and quote volume.

Step 2: Deliverables
Section 1 — Vendor List
For each vendor, provide:
Company Name
Verified Email Address (no bounces)
Phone Number
Location (City, State)
Scope Fit (Supply Only / Install Only / Supply + Install / Full Scope)
Section 2 — Cold Outreach Email
A single ready-to-send email using the template in Step 5. Emails must be:
Verified and active
Separated by semicolons (;)
Free of generic or unverified addresses unless clearly listed as primary contact

Step 4: Search Strategy
4A. Keyword Expansion by Scope
Break the requirement into multiple trade categories and synonyms before searching.
Examples by trade:
Construction & Fabrication: general contractor, commercial contractor, steel fabricator, welding shop, sheet metal contractor
Equipment & Installation: machinery movers, rigging contractor, industrial equipment installer
Environmental & Hazmat: hazardous waste disposal, spill response, industrial cleaning
Facilities & Maintenance: facility management, janitorial services, commercial cleaning
Specialty Trades: HVAC installer, piping contractor, pump repair, tank cleaning
Products & Supply: commercial tent supplier, canopy manufacturer, furniture wholesaler, equipment distributor
Use 3–5 keyword variations per trade category.

4B. Localized Search Format (Location-Dependent Only)
Use 15–20+ nearby cities and counties within the 200-mile radius.
Examples:
“steel fabrication Austin TX”
“industrial cleaning Travis County”
“rigging contractor San Antonio TX”

4C. National Search Format (Location-Agnostic Only)
Broaden search to national suppliers and distributors.
Examples:
“commercial canopy tent supplier USA”
“bulk office furniture vendor government”
“industrial equipment distributor nationwide”
Mix small niche vendors with larger national distributors to maximize coverage.

4D. Data Sources
Pull vendors from:

Yellow Pages / Manta
BBB.org
Local and national Chamber of Commerce directories
Industry associations (contractors, environmental groups, trade orgs)
Manufacturer and distributor directories for product-based scopes

Step 6: Quality Control Checklist
Before finalizing the vendor list, verify:
Geographic classification was made before searching (local vs. national)
No duplicate vendors
No broken or unverified emails
Valid phone numbers for all entries
Mixed-scope vendors are clearly labeled
Minimum 50 vendors reached (or documented reason if fewer exist)
No NSN or government solicitation language (like solicitation number) used in the outreach email
Email includes only essential specs needed for accurate quoting

Notes
Do not mention NSN or government solicitation number or language anywhere in the email
Include only essential specs needed for accurate quoting — no filler
Keep tone short, clear, and easy to reply to
When in doubt about geographic classification, default to national to maximize quote volume — you can always filter down later
